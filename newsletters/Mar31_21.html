What is a flight surgeon?

Physicians in aerospace medicine, commonly referred to as flight surgeons, provide preventative and comprehensive medical care to patients heavily involved in air and space travel (i.e., pilots and astronauts!). Their primary job is to prevent, diagnose, and treat conditions that result from air and/or space travel. This includes everything from the physiology of flight, or how the body is changing as a result of the air or space environment to studying the treatment of work-related conditions, illnesses, or diseases. A flight surgeon may have to deal with medical issues across multiple disciplines, but most commonly practices ophthalmology, pulmonology, orthopedics, dermatology, and even psychiatry.

How do you become a flight surgeon?

Aerospace medicine is a specialty within occupational medicine. Aerospace physicians complete standard medical school (MD or DO degree), then a residency program in aerospace medicine. The five accredited programs in the United States include:

  • Air Force: Wright-Patterson AFB (OH), Military Program
  • Army/Navy: Naval Aerospace Medical Institute (Pensacola, FL), Military Program
  • Wright State University (Dayton, OH), Civilian Program
  • The University of Texas-Medical Branch (Galveston, TX), Civilian Program
  • Mayo Clinic (Rochester, MN), Civilian Program
