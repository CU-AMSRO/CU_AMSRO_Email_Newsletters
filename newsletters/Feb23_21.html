Getting involved in aerospace medicine engineering

There are many opportunities to get more involved in engineering in aerospace medicine. Take a lot at the opportunities below to build your network, compete in a student project, or get a jumpstart on your career! While most of these opportunities are geared specifically towards engineering in aerospace medicine, remember to look broadly for further opportunities where you can build your engineering or medicine skills, or connect with members of those communities!

Student Groups:

Student groups are a great way to meet like-minded peers. Some groups compete in student design competitions or work towards the goals of a larger organization.
  • CUTEE Club: The CU Technologies for Extreme Environments Club competes in the NASA SUITS Challenge. This team works to develop an augmented reality system to assist astronauts during extra-vehicular activity.
  • Engineers without Borders: The CU Chapter of Engineers without Borders works to design sustainable solutions to help communities meet their basic needs, such as access to health care.
  • Engineers in Medicine: A community for engineers interested in working in healthcare.
  • Student chapters of national groups such as AsMA (Aerospace Medical Association) and AIAA (American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics) provide great networking and scholarship opportunities across schools nationwide!
You can always create your own student group to compete in an engineering challenge!

Internships and Co-Ops:

Internships and co-ops are a great way to get hands-on experience in the field. Internships typically occur over one semester, while co-ops occur over two semesters. These opportunities are highly recommended for anyone looking to work in the industry after graduation! Most students aim to do an internship in a summer semester between classes, which makes these terms highly competitive; try aiming for a fall or spring internship to get a unique opportunity.
  • NASA Internships: NASA has many internship opportunities available year-round, with many aerospace medicine projects! Applications for all NASA centers are accepted through the same portal.
  • NASA Pathways: Pathways is NASA's co-op program, which directly leads to employment after successful completion of your terms. Pathways positions are posted for each academic major and NASA center. Pathways positions are only posted for two weeks of the year, so keep an eye out for announcements!
  • Career fairs are also great ways to find internship opportunities. Many human spaceflight companies also post internship applications on their website's careers section. Here are a few internship programs to check out at various human spaceflight companies:
    • SpaceX
    • Blue Origin
    • Lockheed Martin
    • Sierra Nevada
    • Paragon Space
    • Draper
    • MIT Lincoln labs
