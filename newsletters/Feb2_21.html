Speaker Highlight - Dr. Christine Fanchiang

Dr. Christine Fanchiang is a graduate from the University of Colorado Boulder (MS 2010, PhD 2017) who currently works full time as the CEO and chief scientist of The Space Research Company (TSRCo), an aerospace firm primarily focused on developing technologies for advancing human spaceflight. She works on developing small satellites for biology research as well as conducting basic human performance modeling research for spaceflight systems. She has worked on several space projects starting as an undergrad at MIT, where she helped design and build the power system for a walking Mars rover for the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL). At Northrop Grumman, she worked alongside the program manager on a national weather satellite system (NPOESS), coordinating the management, engineering and customer teams. She helped establish and implement a new project track at the University of Colorado for building remotely-operated robotic gardens for future lunar and Martian greenhouses. Additionally, she worked at BioServe Space Technologies to design, build, and launch payload hardware for various space life science experiments on STS-132, STS-133, and STS-135. Her PhD thesis work centered around spacecraft design analysis in the context of astronaut performance and was funded by the NASA Harriet Jenkins Graduate Fellowship.

What is CU AMSRO?

  • Our Mission: To advance the science and art of aerospace medicine among undergraduate, graduate, medical, allied medical, and nursing students, as well as among medical residents, throughout Colorado.

  • Who are we?:
    • Undergraduates
    • Graduate students
    • Medical students
    • Medical residents
    • Allied health students (pharmacy, nursing, occupational therapy, etc.)

  • What do we do?:
    • Skill development through seminar series and workshops
    • Educational opportunities through research partnerships
    • Interdisciplinary networking with students, professors, and industry professionals
    • Community building within and outside of CU

  • Why should you join?: You have an interest in aerospace medicine and/or related topics, want to learn more about the intersection of space and physiology, or want to meet others with similar interests!
