Welcome back CU AMSRO Buffs!

Welcome back to our returning CU AMSRO Buffs, and a hearty welcome to our new members! We are so excited that you have shown interest in the Aerospace Medicine and Student Resident Organization (AMSRO)! This email will give you an overview of who we are, what we do, and the general gist of what events we may be having this semester.

What is CU AMSRO

If you love space, medicine, or the two combined... you are in the right place! AMSRO is a network of people from all backgrounds, including undergraduate and graduate students in engineering and medicine, and so much more. The goal is to 'advocate for aerospace medicine and human performance', provide educational opportunities, and help advance the careers of members.

We are a university chapter part which is part of a national organization, the Aerospace Medicine Student Resident Organization. Membership in the national chapter is not required for you to be a member of the CU chapter!

Basic Life Support Training

We are also planning on holding an American Heart Association Basic Life Support (BLS) class this semester for our members. This class will teach you how to perform CPR and other basic cardiovascular life support skills. The class will most likely be held in a hybrid model - students would study the academics & watch the videos at home individually. The in-person session would involve skill check out, scenarios, & a final test. Your certification from this class will be valid for two years with the potential to renew afterwards.
