Engineers play an important role in the aerospace medicine field by designing innovative solutions to aerospace medicine problems. The engineering design process is used to translate results from research into commercial products. A crucial part of this process is defining requirements. This is especially important when designing solutions around the human body, due to the many differences in the human population. A design is then developed to meet the requirements, and then tested against the requirements prior to being finalized. This process is highly iterative and can be repeated numerous times until the product is finalized. Engineering in aerospace medicine is not only limited to medical devices, but can play a bigger role in the development of large aerospace systems. For example, engineers are needed to develop NASA’s upcoming Human Landing System for our return to the Moon, including the flight control, environmental, and occupant protection systems.
There are many different engineering fields that can contribute to aerospace medicine, such as:

  • Mechanical Engineering

    ⇒ Example: Exercise devices for long-duration spaceflight

  • Chemical Engineering

    ⇒ Example: CO2 scrubbing technologies

  • Biomedical Engineering

    ⇒ Example: Occupant protection models

  • Electrical Engineering

    ⇒ Example: Astronaut health monitoring devices

  • Systems Engineering

    ⇒ Example: Exploration Medical Capability

  • Materials Science and Engineering

    ⇒ Example: Radiation protection materials

  • Aerospace Engineering

    ⇒ Example: Optimal manual flight control algorithms for spatial disorientation


While engineers will specialize in one of these fields, they will rely on knowledge from the other fields to achieve their design goals.
