"I'm interested in aerospace medicine research, how do I get more involved?"

The aerospace medicine research community spans a range of institutions such as universities, national laboratories, and private companies. In this week’s update, we share ideas for careers in this field, as well as ways to keep up with the community at different stages of your education.

For undergraduates:

  • Majors such as Integrative Physiology, Biology, Psychology, or Aerospace, Mechanical, and Biomedical Engineering all provide relevant backgrounds.
  • Research experiences with faculty at CU or other universities are valuable - check out the NSF's REU and SURF programs!

For graduate students:

  • Funding opportunities for graduate study in aerospace medicine include the NSF, NSTGRO, NDSEG, SMART, and Draper fellowships, in addition to other major national fellowships.

For all students:

  • Consider joining professional societies as a student member - AsMA, AIAA, and HFES all offer newsletters, community message boards, and (importantly) scholarship opportunities!

Research Highlight

Sarah Leary has worked as a research assistant developing a tool for repeatable placement of electrocardiogram (ECG) electrodes for cardiac health monitoring in operational environments. Sarah’s work showcases the blend of engineering and medicine required in aerospace medicine research.
